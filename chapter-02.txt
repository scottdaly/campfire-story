CHAPTER TWO — Morning

Morning came gray and dripping. They sat around the crackling fire
from the night before, passing the one dry box of crackers, and
took inventory.

The map was fine. The chili was a loss. And the photograph — the
whole reason for the trip — was not in the map case where Sam had
laminated it, sworn on his life it was.

"Somebody repacked," Sam said.

Biscuit, who had slept through the storm like a furred stone, yawned
enormously and said nothing.
