CHAPTER THREE — The Long Portage

The portage trail was marked on the map as eight hundred meters.
The map was a liar.

They carried the canoes in shifts, Biscuit supervising from
underfoot, and came out at last above a second lake. The lake was
quiet. Somewhere across it, if the photograph could be trusted —
and it could not, being lost — was the campsite where the whole
thing had started.

Sam checked the map case again, out of habit. Then he checked the
chili pot, out of desperation. And there, folded twice and
chili-stained at one corner, was the photograph.

"Somebody repacked," Dee said.
